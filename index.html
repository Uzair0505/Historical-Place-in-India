Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum on the southern bank of the river Yamuna in the Indian city of Agra. It was commissioned in 1632 by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan to house the tomb of his wife Mumtaz Mahal.

Red Fort

The Red Fort is a historic fort in the city of Delhi. It was the main residence of the Mughal emperors. Shah Jahan commissioned the construction in 1638 when he shifted the capital from Agra to Delhi.

Qutub Minar

The Qutub Minar is a minaret that forms part of the Qutb complex, a UNESCO World Heritage Site in Delhi. It was built in 1193 by Qutb-ud-din Aibak, marking the beginning of Muslim rule in India.

Hawa Mahal

Hawa Mahal is a palace in Jaipur, built with red and pink sandstone. It is located on the edge of the City Palace, and was designed to allow royal ladies to observe street festivals unseen.

India Gate

India Gate is a war memorial located astride the Rajpath in New Delhi, built in memory of soldiers of the British Indian Army who died during World War I. It was designed by Sir Edwin Lutyens and stands at 42 meters high.
